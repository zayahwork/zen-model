Overview

Teaching an AI
to hold a star steady.

Fusion is how the sun makes energy. On Earth, the barrier is the plasma — superheated matter that wants to go unstable and crash. ZEN Model is an AI that learns to control that plasma: pushing it hot for more energy while steering it away from collapse.

It doesn't run on a hand-made simulator. First a neural network learned how real plasma evolves, from 326 shots of real MAST tokamak data. That learned world model became the reactor the controller trains inside.

The controller was trained with PPO reinforcement learning — playing the dream reactor thousands of times, rewarded for keeping plasma hot and alive, penalized for crashes.

Two modes emerged. Safe Mode holds plasma stable the entire run. Risk Mode pushes temperature higher for more simulated energy, but rides the edge and eventually disrupts.

How it actually worked

Six honest steps.

01

Real reactor data

Pulled 326 real shots from the MAST tokamak (UK) — plasma temperature and current over time, straight from the experimental archive. No synthetic data.

02

Forecast the plasma

Trained an LSTM, then a Transformer, to predict the plasma's next moves. Tested only on shots the model had never seen, with by-shot splits to prevent leakage.

Found: Transformer hit R² 0.836, beating persistence, linear regression, MLP and LSTM. Adding more signals (current, heating) didn't help — recent temperature already contained their effect.
03

Add physics

Built a physics-informed loss (a PINN) that penalizes physically-impossible jumps, so predictions respect how plasma actually behaves.

Found: same accuracy, but measurably smoother, more physical forecasts — the real point of a PINN.
04

Detect disruptions

A first detector using temperature flagged 95% of shots — clearly broken. Switched to plasma-current collapse, the true signature of a disruption.

Lesson: caught my own bad labels instead of building on them. That check is the science.
05

Build a world model

Turned the trained predictor into a "dream reactor" — a learned environment driven by real plasma dynamics, where a controller could safely practice.

Trap caught: an early controller exploited errors in the world model (driving temp negative to fake survival). Constrained the dream to force real control.
06

Learn to control it

Trained a PPO controller inside the dream. An early version reward-hacked — grabbing heat points then crashing. Fixed the reward to prioritize survival, then found the real ceiling of hot-and-stable.

Result: the only strategy with 100% survival, holding ~2× the temperature of safe-but-cold play.

Honest scope

What it is —
and what it isn't.

This is a proof of concept, in simulation, on one retired experimental reactor. The plasma dynamics come from real MAST data; the instability and control layer are simplified. It demonstrates the method real fusion labs use — prediction plus reinforcement-learned control — but it is not a deployable reactor controller. The honesty is the point: overclaiming would make it less credible, not more.
